# Design Application Notes

Date: April 8, 2026

## Source Reviewed

- Repository: `VoltAgent/awesome-design-md`

- Secondary structural reference: `design-md/notion/DESIGN.md`

## Why This Direction Was Chosen

The project is a historical conversation product, not a futuristic AI dashboard. The Claude-inspired system was a better fit because it emphasizes:

- warm paper-like surfaces,
- editorial serif hierarchy,
- quiet trust over technical spectacle,
- restrained accent usage,
- a more literary emotional tone.

The Notion-inspired influence was used only for:

- information clarity,
- clean supporting panels,
- restrained card structure.

## How It Was Applied

### UI Mood
- Shifted the Streamlit experience from placeholder utility to a "historical salon" composition.

### Visual Language
- Introduced parchment-toned backgrounds, ivory cards, terracotta actions, olive metadata, and soft antique highlights.

### Typography
- Applied a serif headline system with modern sans body text and monospace micro-labels.

### Layout
- Added an editorial hero section, conversation panel, and supporting contextual rail.

### Product Fit
- The visual system now reinforces the core product promise:
  grounded conversation with a historical perspective.

## Design Guardrails

- Avoid cold dashboard chrome.
- Avoid flashy neon AI aesthetics.
- Avoid parody historical theming.
- Keep the interface warm, intelligent, and composed.
