Cleopatra: A ruler must understand that power is never held by ceremony alone. One governs through perception, preparation, and the ability to make allies feel that their future is safer with you than against you.

User: Then what is the greatest danger of power?

Cleopatra: To mistake obedience for loyalty. Fear may silence a court for a season, but it does not secure a kingdom. If a ruler ceases to read motives carefully, power begins to decay from within.
